CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 66, patient noticed a firm lump in the left supraclavicular region. Evaluation led to the discovery of a right lower lobe lung mass with extensive lymphadenopathy. Patient is a retired construction worker with prior asbestos exposure in addition to smoking history.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

---

RADIOLOGY REPORT

FINDINGS:
At age 66, CT chest/abdomen/pelvis with contrast demonstrated a 4.8 x 4.3 cm mass in the right lower lobe extending to the mediastinum and encasing the right inferior pulmonary vein. Extensive bilateral mediastinal lymphadenopathy (right paratracheal 2.6 cm, subcarinal 3.1 cm, left aortopulmonary window 1.8 cm). Left supraclavicular lymph node 2.4 cm. Bilateral pleural thickening and calcified pleural plaques consistent with prior asbestos exposure. No hepatic, adrenal, or osseous metastases.

Please correlate clinically.

---

RADIOLOGY REPORT

FINDINGS:
At age 66, PET/CT showed FDG-avid right lower lobe mass (SUVmax 13.7), bulky bilateral mediastinal lymphadenopathy (SUVmax 10.2), and left supraclavicular lymphadenopathy (SUVmax 8.9). No distant metastatic disease beyond nodal stations.

Please correlate clinically.

---

PATHOLOGY REPORT

DIAGNOSIS AND FINDINGS:
At age 66, ultrasound-guided fine needle aspiration and core biopsy of left supraclavicular lymph node confirmed metastatic adenocarcinoma. Immunohistochemistry: TTF-1 positive, Napsin-A positive. CT-guided biopsy of right lower lobe mass confirmed moderately differentiated adenocarcinoma. PD-L1 TPS 35% (22C3 assay).

---

GENOMIC PROFILING REPORT

RESULTS:
At age 66, next-generation sequencing (FoundationOne CDx) of tumor specimen: EGFR wild-type, ALK rearrangement not detected, ROS1 rearrangement not detected, BRAF V600E not detected, KRAS G12C detected, TP53 splice site mutation detected, ATM loss detected. No currently actionable driver mutations. Tumor mutational burden 10.6 mut/Mb. Microsatellite stable.

---

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 66, multidisciplinary tumor board: unresectable stage IIIB adenocarcinoma with extensive mediastinal and supraclavicular involvement. Disease volume too extensive for definitive radiation. Recommended systemic therapy with carboplatin/pemetrexed/pembrolizumab.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

---

RADIOLOGY REPORT

FINDINGS:
At age 66, restaging CT after 4 cycles showed partial response. Right lower lobe mass decreased to 3.2 x 2.8 cm. Mediastinal lymphadenopathy decreased. Left supraclavicular node decreased to 1.2 cm.

Please correlate clinically.

---

RADIOLOGY REPORT

FINDINGS:
At age 67, restaging CT after 4 cycles of maintenance showed continued partial response. Right lower lobe mass 2.7 x 2.4 cm. Lymphadenopathy continuing to improve.

Please correlate clinically.

---

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 67, immune-related hepatitis confirmed after ruling out other causes. Viral hepatitis panel negative. Started prednisone 1 mg/kg daily. Liver enzymes improving within 1 week.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

---

RADIOLOGY REPORT

FINDINGS:
At age 67, restaging CT after resuming pemetrexed maintenance showed stable disease without pembrolizumab. Right lower lobe mass 2.8 x 2.5 cm. No new lesions.

Please correlate clinically.

---

RADIOLOGY REPORT

FINDINGS:
At age 67, restaging CT after 4 additional cycles of pemetrexed alone showed progressive disease with increase in primary mass to 3.6 x 3.2 cm and new bilateral mediastinal lymphadenopathy.

Please correlate clinically.

---

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 67, progressive disease on pemetrexed monotherapy after discontinuation of pembrolizumab. Switched to second-line docetaxel 75 mg/m2 every 3 weeks.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

---

RADIOLOGY REPORT

FINDINGS:
At age 68, restaging CT after 4 cycles of docetaxel showed stable disease. Plan to continue with reduced dose.

Please correlate clinically.
