CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 58, initial laboratory evaluation showed white blood cell count of 3,200/uL with 22% circulating blasts, hemoglobin 6.8 g/dL, and platelet count of 34,000/uL. LDH was elevated at 890 U/L. Coagulation studies were within normal limits.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

---

PATHOLOGY REPORT

DIAGNOSIS AND FINDINGS:
At age 58, bone marrow biopsy with aspirate revealed a hypercellular marrow (90% cellularity) with 64% myeloblasts showing partial maturation to promyelocytes. Blasts were positive for CD34, CD117, CD33, CD13, CD15 (partial), HLA-DR, and MPO by flow cytometry.

---

GENOMIC PROFILING REPORT

RESULTS:
At age 58, molecular profiling by next-generation sequencing identified FLT3-ITD mutation with a high allelic ratio of 1.35, DNMT3A R882C mutation, and IDH2 R140Q mutation. NPM1 was wild-type. No TP53 or ASXL1 mutations were detected.

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 58, cytogenetic analysis demonstrated a normal female karyotype, 46,XX, in all 20 metaphases. ELN 2022 risk classification was adverse based on FLT3-ITD positivity in the absence of NPM1 mutation.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

PATHOLOGY REPORT

DIAGNOSIS AND FINDINGS:
At age 58, day 14 bone marrow biopsy showed a hypocellular marrow (15% cellularity) with 18% residual blasts, indicating a suboptimal early response.

---

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 58, given persistent blasts on day 14, the decision was made to proceed with re-induction using the 5+2 regimen with cytarabine for five days and daunorubicin for two days, again combined with midostaurin.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

---

PATHOLOGY REPORT

DIAGNOSIS AND FINDINGS:
At age 58, day 28 bone marrow biopsy following re-induction showed 2.4% blasts by morphology, meeting criteria for complete remission. However, flow cytometry MRD was positive at 0.32%, indicating measurable residual disease.

---

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 58, neutrophil engraftment occurred on day plus 18. Day plus 30 bone marrow showed complete donor chimerism and no detectable disease.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 58, GVHD improved with combination therapy and the patient was discharged on a steroid taper and ruxolitinib maintenance. Day plus 100 marrow remained in complete remission with undetectable MRD by flow cytometry.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 59, approximately eight months post-transplant, routine CBC showed recurrence of circulating blasts at 4%. Bone marrow biopsy confirmed morphologic relapse with 38% blasts harboring the original FLT3-ITD mutation.

The patient was seen in clinic today for ongoing oncologic management.

CLINICAL PROGRESS NOTE

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
At age 59, after two cycles of gilteritinib the patient achieved a second complete remission with 1.2% blasts and is being considered for donor lymphocyte infusion to augment graft-versus-leukemia effect.
