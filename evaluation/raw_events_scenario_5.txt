<pathology_report>At age 34, excisional biopsy of the right lateral neck lesion revealed malignant melanoma with Breslow thickness of 1.5 mm, Clark level III, without ulceration, mitotic rate 2/mm2, no microsatellites, no lymphovascular invasion, and negative margins on excisional biopsy.
<surgery>At age 34, wide local excision with 1-2 cm margins of the right neck melanoma was performed with primary closure.
<surgery>At age 34, sentinel lymph node biopsy of the right cervical chain showed 1 of 2 sentinel nodes positive for a 1.2 mm metastatic melanoma deposit.
<pathology_report>At age 34, sentinel lymph node pathology confirmed metastatic melanoma in 1 of 2 nodes (1.2 mm deposit), no extracapsular extension, S100 and Melan-A positive.
<imaging_report>At age 34, staging MRI brain with gadolinium revealed a solitary 2.5 cm enhancing mass in the left occipital lobe with surrounding vasogenic edema consistent with brain metastasis.
<imaging_report>At age 34, staging PET/CT showed FDG-avid right cervical node (post-biopsy) and no other extracranial metastatic disease.
<ngs_report>At age 34, next-generation sequencing of the primary melanoma revealed BRAF V600E mutation (allele frequency 45%), NRAS wild-type, wild-type TP53, TERT promoter wild-type, and tumor mutational burden of 6 mutations per megabase with PD-L1 combined positive score of 8.
<clinical_note>At age 34, serum LDH was normal at 170 U/L despite brain metastasis, and the patient had ECOG performance status of 0 with no neurological deficits aside from mild left-sided visual field changes.
<radiation>At age 34, stereotactic radiosurgery was delivered to the left occipital brain metastasis at 22 Gy in 1 fraction with frameless technique.
<systemic>At age 34, combination ipilimumab 3 mg/kg plus nivolumab 1 mg/kg IV every 3 weeks was initiated for 4 cycles for BRAF V600E melanoma with brain metastasis, with immunotherapy chosen first per CheckMate 204 data.
<adverse_event>At age 34, patient developed grade 2 immune-related colitis with watery diarrhea (6 stools/day) after cycle 3, managed with oral prednisone 1 mg/kg and 1 cycle delay of therapy.
<adverse_event>At age 34, patient developed grade 1 immune-related thyroiditis with transient thyrotoxicosis (TSH <0.01 mIU/L, free T4 2.8 ng/dL) evolving to hypothyroidism requiring levothyroxine supplementation.
<systemic>At age 34, after completing 4 induction cycles of ipilimumab/nivolumab, nivolumab 480 mg IV every 4 weeks was continued as maintenance.
<imaging_report>At age 34, restaging MRI brain at 12 weeks showed significant decrease in the left occipital lesion to 0.8 cm with decreased surrounding edema, consistent with treatment response.
<imaging_report>At age 34, restaging CT chest/abdomen/pelvis at 12 weeks showed no extracranial metastatic disease.
<clinical_note>At age 35, LDH remained normal at 158 U/L and patient had complete resolution of visual field deficits.
<imaging_report>At age 35, 6-month MRI brain showed continued response with the left occipital lesion now measuring 0.4 cm with peripheral enhancement likely representing treatment effect.
<imaging_report>At age 35, 6-month PET/CT showed no extracranial metastatic disease.
<clinical_note>At age 35, patient continued nivolumab maintenance with excellent intracranial and extracranial disease control and ECOG performance status of 0, with plan to continue for up to 2 years.
<imaging_report>At age 35, 12-month MRI brain showed near complete resolution of the left occipital lesion with only minimal residual enhancement, and no new intracranial lesions.
<clinical_note>At age 36, after 2 years of total immunotherapy (induction plus maintenance), nivolumab was electively discontinued given sustained complete intracranial and extracranial response with LDH normal at 149 U/L.
